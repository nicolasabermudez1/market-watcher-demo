## 1. Category Overview

Cloud Infrastructure encompasses all Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS) and Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS) consumption across Centrica Group, including British Gas, Bord Gáis Energy, and Hive. The category covers compute, storage, networking, managed Kubernetes, disaster recovery, and data platform services delivered via the three PSL-approved hyperscalers: Microsoft Azure, Amazon Web Services (AWS), and Google Cloud Platform (GCP).

**Total addressable spend (2024/25 actuals):** £8.1m  
**Forecast (2025/26):** £9.4m (+16% driven by AI/ML workloads and smart-meter data platform migration)  
**Number of active suppliers:** 3 (PSL-approved hyperscalers) + 6 specialist resellers/managed service providers

## 3. Supply Market Analysis

### 3.1 Market Structure
The global cloud IaaS/PaaS market is highly concentrated. Microsoft Azure and AWS collectively hold ~55% market share. GCP is the clear third at ~12%. All three are investment-grade, financially resilient, and operationally stable. The key market dynamics relevant to Centrica are:

- **AI compute demand** is driving hyperscaler capacity expansion globally; UK-region capacity is tightening, with Azure UK South near saturation at peak.
- **Regulatory fragmentation** between UK post-Brexit and EU cloud frameworks creates compliance overhead — particularly for cross-border data flows under the UK GDPR adequacy decision.
- **Pricing pressure** is shifting: committed-use and reserved-instance models now require 1–3 year commitments to achieve material discounts (25–40%), creating tension with Centrica's IT agility mandate.

### 3.2 Competitive Dynamics
Microsoft benefits from the embedded M365/Azure hybrid — switching cost is high. AWS has the deepest engineering talent pool and broadest service catalogue. GCP is increasingly competitive on data/AI tooling and pricing for new workloads. All three are expanding UK data centre footprints.
